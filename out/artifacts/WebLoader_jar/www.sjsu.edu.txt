                     <h3>Campus Tour</h3>
                     <div class="campusTour">
                        <p><a class="colorboxSlide tourLocations" href="/sjsuhome/pics/tower-hall.jpg" title="Tower Hall: Tower Hall is the centerpiece of San Jose State University. Built in 1910 to replace a previous building damaged in the earthquake of 1906, Tower Hall is the oldest structure on the SJSU campus, the oldest public school of higher education in California. State architects Sellon &amp; Hemmings designed the building in the Spanish Revival style with influences from Gothic, Renaissance Revival, and Modern, reflecting the rich diversity found in San Jose."><img src="/sjsuhome/pics/homepage-campus-tour-01.jpg" alt="Tower Hall"></a></p>
                        <p class="acc"><a class="colorboxSlide tourLocations" href="/sjsuhome/pics/library-03.jpg" title="In 2003 San José State University and the City of San Jose came together to form a unique partnership offering joint library services to citizens of San Jose and SJSU students and faculty. The Martin Luther King, Junior Library on corner of 4th and East San Fernando Streets, features the world renowned Ira F. Brilliant Center for Beethoven Studies and Martha Heasley Cox Steinbeck Research Center."></a></p>
                        <p class="acc"><a class="colorboxSlide tourLocations" href="/sjsuhome/pics/statues-02.jpg" title="Smith/Carlos Statue: SJSU's iconic Tommie Smith and John Carlos sculpture honors the two 1968 Olympic gold and bronze medal winners and SJSU student activists.  Designed by artist Rigo 23, the sculpture depicts the pivotal moment in history when Smith and Carlos took a stand for human rights on the victory podium at the Olympics, a silent protest that was seen around the world. The sculpture stands 20-feet tall and is situated in the Sculpture Garden between Clark Hall and Tower Hall."></a></p>
                        <p class="acc"><a class="colorboxSlide tourLocations" href="/sjsuhome/pics/campus_building.jpg" title="The Central Classroom Building is home to SJSU’s Nutrition, Food Science, and Packaging Department, the first nutrition department in the California State University (CSU) system. Department faculty have received awards for their outstanding research and scholarly activities including “Healthy San Jose State,” a campus-wide initiative aimed at increasing healthy eating and active living among students, faculty and staff at SJSU. Thanks to the initiative the SJSU community now has the Spartan Smart Cart, a food stand selling healthy snacks, fresh fruits, and organic items at the north side of the Central Classroom Building during fall and spring terms."></a></p>
                        <p class="acc"><a class="colorboxSlide tourLocations" href="/sjsuhome/pics/housing.jpg" title="Campus Village: Campus Village is located on the south-east corner of campus, and is a short walking distance to classes, and Downtown San Jose.  The Village Market offers a variety of eateries and a convenience store for drinks and snacks."></a></p>
                        <p class="acc"><a class="colorboxSlide tourLocations" href="/sjsuhome/pics/chavez_arch.jpg" title="Memorial Arch: The memorial arch to César E. Chávez along the Paseo de César E. Chávez is an eloquent testament to Chávez's struggles and achievements and an inspiration to others to follow a life of service. Designed by acclaimed muralist Judith F. Baca, the monument is a 22-foot tall arch topped with a glass eagle, the United Farm Workers' emblem, with decorative murals depicting people, concepts, symbols and historical events central to Chávez's life and the movement he led."></a></p>
                        <p class="acc"><a class="colorboxSlide tourLocations" href="/sjsuhome/pics/business_building.jpg" title="Lucas College and Graduate School of Business: For more than 80 years SJSU's Lucas College and Graduate School of Business has prepared future business leaders. The college builds on Silicon Valley's innovation and high-tech location, while focusing on global issues to prepare our students for success in today's ever-changing marketplace.  Undergraduate and graduate programs at the Lucas College and Graduate School of Business are accredited by AACSB International, a distinguished hallmark of excellence in business education earned by less than 5% of business programs worldwide."></a></p>
